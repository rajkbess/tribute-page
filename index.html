Brendan Eich


The creator of JavaScript

Co-founder of Mozila and former Cheif Executive Officer, now the Cheif Executive Officer at Brave Software

Simple timeline of his carier/life

  • July 4,1961 - Born.
  • 1979 - graduated at Ellwood P. Cubberley High School.
  • Bachelor's degree at Santa Clara University.
  • 1985 - Master's degree at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.
  • April 1995 - He started work at Netscape Communications Corporation.
  • September 1995 - LiveScript was realesed or now known as JavaScript.
  • 1998 - Eich co-founded the Mozilla project with Mitchell Baker, creating the website mozilla.org that was meant to manage open-source contributions to the Netscape source code
  • August 2005 - Eich became CTO of the newly founded Mozilla Corporation, meant to be the Mozilla Foundation's for-profit arm.
  • March 24, 2014 - Eich was promoted to CEO of Mozilla Corporation
  • April 3, 2014 - Eich stepped down as CEO and resigned from working at Mozilla.
  • January 20, 2016 - Brave Software released developer versions of its open-source Brave web browser, which blocked ads and trackers and included a micropayments system to offer users a choice between viewing selected ads or paying websites not to display them.
